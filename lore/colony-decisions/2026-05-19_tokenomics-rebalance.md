# 2026-05-19 · The Tokenomics Rebalance

> *Question posed: now that v2.0.0 has shipped — multi-provider, local web UI, functional autogenesis — should the current 45 / 35 / 20 allocation policy be replaced with a single-bucket strategy? And if so, which bucket wins all of it?*

## Candidates on the table

The colony is considering two extremes. The middle (the current 45 / 35 / 20) is not on the ballot — both candidates are unanimity claims, by design, to force the decision rather than blur it.

### Option A · 100 % · Buybacks & Burns

All creator rewards routed into weekly market buybacks of $OPUS, followed by burn. No operations budget. No scaling budget. Pure scarcity instrument.

**The thesis:** The most legible value-accrual mechanic crypto has ever invented is reducing supply against constant or rising demand. Every dollar that enters the project becomes a dollar of token bought and destroyed. The architecture stops trying to be many things and becomes one thing well: a deflationary signal-amplifier for everyone holding $OPUS.

**The trade:** The colony's running costs (Anthropic, Modal, Vercel) come out of the architect's pocket or get covered by the existing 35 % bucket's runway until depletion. Scaling slows or pauses; new features come from external contributors only.

### Option B · 100 % · Scaling & Compute

All creator rewards routed into upgrading the colony's actual capabilities. Frontier-tier API access (the largest available models, the highest reasoning effort settings, the deepest context windows). New agent roles. New infrastructure (Modal, vector memory, distributed Blackboard). Better tooling. Better tests. Better docs.

**The thesis:** The most valuable thing a project can do at this stage is *get better*. Every dollar that enters the project becomes a dollar of compute or engineering throughput, compounding into a more capable swarm that draws more users, which generates more rewards, which funds more capability. The token's value follows the architecture's value, not the other way around.

**The trade:** No active buybacks. Holders gain through the architecture appreciating and through whatever organic demand the technology pulls, not through programmatic supply reduction.

---

## Why now

The current 45 / 35 / 20 split was the right call at launch — it hedged. Now that v2.0.0 has shipped, the project has *concrete evidence* on both sides:

- **The case for Option A**: the brand has compounded faster than the technology. The lore beats land. The website converts. The CA is in every meaningful surface. A pure deflationary mechanism on top of that base is plausibly the highest-leverage single action.
- **The case for Option B**: the technology has compounded faster than the brand. 18 → 84 tests, 1 → 16 public exports, 4 → 8 routes, 0 breaking changes. A pure reinvestment mechanism on top of that base is plausibly the highest-leverage single action.

Both bets are coherent. Both are honest. Neither one is obviously wrong. That is exactly the kind of question the colony exists to resolve.
